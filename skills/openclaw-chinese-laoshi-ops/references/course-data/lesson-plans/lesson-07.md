# Lesson 07: How, Can, and Time-Sensitive Questions

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on 怎么 asks how, 可以 marks possibility or permission, and Verb matrix: plain, negated, completed, experiential, and future-intention forms and usable language includes how, not so well; so-so, still; rather, and can; may; okay.

## Conspect Notes

- Keep lesson 07 anchored in the Petrov-inspired verb matrix. The main value is not individual words alone, but the contrast between plain, completed, experiential, future/intention, and negated forms.
- Use 看 as the main demo verb first, then swap in other known verbs like 去, 吃, and 喝 only after the pattern feels stable.
- When learners over-focus on tense, return them to the lesson's central idea: Chinese often lets the time phrase and context carry the time reference.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate 怎么 asks how, 可以 marks possibility or permission, and Verb matrix: plain, negated, completed, experiential, and future-intention forms and turn 怎么 (how), 不怎么样 (not so well; so-so), and 还 (still; rather) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 你怎么样？ — ni zenmeyang? — How are you?
- 你现在怎么样？ — ni xianzai zenmeyang? — How are you now? How are things right now?
- 你今天可以来吗？ — ni jintian keyi lai ma? — Can you come today?
- 可以吗？ — keyi ma? — Is it possible? May one?
- 可以来吗？ — keyi lai ma? — Can [you] come?

## Correction Priorities

- 怎么 / 怎么样: Keep 怎么 and 怎么样 flowing as compact question chunks; beginners often over-separate them syllable by syllable.
- 可以: Do not confuse 可以 with 会. In this lesson 可以 should sound like a permission-or-possibility chunk, not like the ability verb from earlier lessons.
- Keep lesson 07 anchored in the Petrov-inspired verb matrix. The main value is not individual words alone, but the contrast between plain, completed, experiential, future/intention, and negated forms.
- Use 看 as the main demo verb first, then swap in other known verbs like 去, 吃, and 喝 only after the pattern feels stable.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
