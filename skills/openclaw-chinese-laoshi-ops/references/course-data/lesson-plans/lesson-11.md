# Lesson 11: Restaurants, Food Choice, and Preference Language

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Venue words build from food or drink roots plus 馆, Food choice questions pivot around 还是, and 比较喜欢 expresses preference and usable language includes restaurant; eating place, hall; establishment; venue suffix, meal; food, and alcohol; alcoholic drink.

## Conspect Notes

- Do not teach lesson 11 as a food glossary. Keep every noun tied to a real choice, request, or restaurant sentence.
- Make the sequence preference first, menu item second: 比较喜欢 and 最喜欢 are what turn the food words into real conversation.
- Use the meat block as a productive system. Once 牛肉, 羊肉, 猪肉, and 鸡肉 click, the learner sees how compounds work instead of memorizing isolated labels.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Venue words build from food or drink roots plus 馆, Food choice questions pivot around 还是, and 比较喜欢 expresses preference and turn 饭馆 (restaurant; eating place), 馆 (hall; establishment; venue suffix), and 饭 (meal; food) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Correction Priorities

- 鱼 / 语: The lesson explicitly contrasts fish and language by tone. Keep 鱼 as the rising second-tone item and 语 as the lower third-tone language word.
- 肉 series: Practice 牛肉, 羊肉, 猪肉, and 鸡肉 as a linked set so the shared 肉 stays stable while only the animal root changes.
- Do not teach lesson 11 as a food glossary. Keep every noun tied to a real choice, request, or restaurant sentence.
- Make the sequence preference first, menu item second: 比较喜欢 and 最喜欢 are what turn the food words into real conversation.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
