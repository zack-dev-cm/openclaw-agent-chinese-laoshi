# Lesson 02: Question Words, Identity, and Place

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Question words stay in slot, Copula 是 for identity, and Name pattern with 叫 and usable language includes what; which, who, where; where to, and to be located in; at.

## Conspect Notes

- Open lesson 02 by reviewing only one grammar expansion at a time: question words, then name, then residence, then nationality, then work.
- Keep student output in very short clauses before combining them into mini self-introductions.
- Use profession words only if they matter to the learner; the lesson itself models this selective vocabulary strategy.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Question words stay in slot, Copula 是 for identity, and Name pattern with 叫 and turn 什么 (what; which), 谁 (who), and 哪儿 (where; where to) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 他是谁？ — ta shi shei? — Who is he?
- 你叫什么名字？ — ni jiao shenme mingzi? — What is your name?
- 你叫什么(名字)? — ni jiao shenme (mingzi)? — What is your name?
- 你住在哪儿？ — ni zhu zai nar? — Where do you live?
- 你是哪国人？ — ni shi na guo ren? — What country are you from? / What nationality are you?

## Correction Priorities

- 什么: Keep the second syllable in 什么 light and unstressed; the question word should not sound like two equal Russian stresses.
- 谁 and 是: Separate 谁 and 是 clearly; Russian ears often blur these into one fuzzy sh-sound cluster.

- Keep student output in very short clauses before combining them into mini self-introductions.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
