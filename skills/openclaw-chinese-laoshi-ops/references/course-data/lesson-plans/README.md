# Lesson Plans

- [Lesson 01](./lesson-01.md): Build the first stable pronoun matrix and automate yes/no etiquette lines.
- [Lesson 02](./lesson-02.md): Open the matrix into question words and personal identification.
- [Lesson 03](./lesson-03.md): Add high-frequency verbs without breaking the original pronoun frame.
- [Lesson 04](./lesson-04.md): Move the same sentence logic into time, dates, and calendar talk.
- [Lesson 05](./lesson-05.md): Stretch the language into larger numbers, direction, and destination statements.
- [Lesson 06](./lesson-06.md): Make time-of-day and knowing-language statements automatic.
- [Lesson 07](./lesson-07.md): Increase flexibility with how / can / still / already style questions.
- [Lesson 08](./lesson-08.md): Turn the room outward into meeting people, age, and quantity dialogue.
- [Lesson 09](./lesson-09.md): Stabilize demonstratives, classifiers, and comparison inside everyday talk.
- [Lesson 10](./lesson-10.md): Push noun + color + classifier handling into shopping dialogue.
- [Lesson 11](./lesson-11.md): Move the learner into restaurant choice and preference exchanges.
- [Lesson 12](./lesson-12.md): Make service language and invitation dialogue polite and fast.
- [Lesson 13](./lesson-13.md): Use dates, money, and experience talk without losing sentence control.
- [Lesson 14](./lesson-14.md): Handle weather, temperature, and travel geography in live questions.
- [Lesson 15](./lesson-15.md): Rework earlier material through polite address, etiquette, and family.
- [Lesson 16](./lesson-16.md): Collapse the whole course into biography, follow-up questions, and closing dialogue.
