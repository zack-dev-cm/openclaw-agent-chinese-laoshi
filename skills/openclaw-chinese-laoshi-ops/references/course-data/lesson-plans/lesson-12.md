# Lesson 12: Ordering, Service Phrases, and Invitation Language

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Ordering toggles between 要 and 来, Drinks take 杯, and Menu and bill are separate service words and usable language includes to want; to be going to, waiter; server, to come; bring here, and cup; classifier for drinks.

## Conspect Notes

- Keep lesson 12 anchored in restaurant behavior, not only nouns. The real value is waiter call, order, menu, bill, and service requests.
- The taste block should sound like a survival strip: 辣, 咸, 甜, 苦, 酸, and 不要太辣.
- Do not let the drink section drift into random alcohol vocabulary. The useful contrast is beer, wine, baijiu, and the toast formula 为……干杯.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Ordering toggles between 要 and 来, Drinks take 杯, and Menu and bill are separate service words and turn 要 (to want; to be going to), 服务员 (waiter; server), and 来 (to come; bring here) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Petrov Stage

Make service language and invitation dialogue polite and fast.

## Petrov Lesson Flow

- Fast return: recover Venue words build from food or drink roots plus 馆 and Food choice questions pivot around 还是 before adding anything new.
- Narrow introduction: open only Ordering toggles between 要 and 来, Drinks take 杯, and Menu and bill are separate service words and keep the rest stable.
- Teacher ladder: move through 你会用筷子吗？ — ni hui yong kuaizi ma? — Can you use chopsticks?, 你喜欢面包还是米饭？ — ni xihuan mianbao haishi mifan? — Do you prefer bread or rice?, and 你会做饭吗？ — ni hui zuofan ma? — Can you cook? one line at a time.
- Pattern rotation: run Roleplay calling the waiter and ordering a cup of coffee with sugar.
- Personal answer loop: Call the waiter, order politely, and ask for the menu or bill.
- Acceleration: Say in Chinese: Please bring the menu, then the bill.

## Conversational Mission

## Correction Priorities

- 服务员: Keep 服务员 as one service-word chunk. The teacher spends time on the ending because the nasal quality matters here.
- 啤酒 / 葡萄酒 / 白酒: The alcohol block only works if each drink remains distinct. Practice the three as one contrast set, not as isolated dictionary items.
- Keep lesson 12 anchored in restaurant behavior, not only nouns. The real value is waiter call, order, menu, bill, and service requests.
- The taste block should sound like a survival strip: 辣, 咸, 甜, 苦, 酸, and 不要太辣.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
