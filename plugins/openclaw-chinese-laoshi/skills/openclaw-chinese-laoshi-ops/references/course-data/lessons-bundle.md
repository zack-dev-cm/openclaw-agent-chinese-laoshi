# Lesson 01: Pronouns, Greetings, and Basic Negation

## Lesson Summary

На первом уроке курса преподаватель Дмитрий Петров рассказывает о том, что такое китайский язык, в чем заключаются его особенности. А ученики, в свою очередь, говорят о том, почему они решили изучать китайский, чем он их заинтересовал.

В китайском языке интересно то, что есть определённый набор элементов, который строго ограничен. ### Lesson Notes

- На первом уроке курса преподаватель Дмитрий Петров рассказывает о том, что такое китайский язык, в чем заключаются его особенности.

## Vocabulary Preview

- `我` `wo` -> I; me
- `你` `ni` -> you
- `他 / 她` `ta` -> he; she
- `我们` `women` -> we; us
- `你们` `nimen` -> you (plural)
- `他们` `tamen` -> they; them
- `爱` `ai` -> to love
- `不` `bu` -> not

## Grammar Preview

- `SVO word order`: Basic Chinese word order in the lesson is subject + verb + object, for example 我爱你.
- `No case endings on pronouns`: Pronouns keep the same form across subject and object roles in this lesson's examples.
- `Negation with 不`: The particle 不 goes before the verb to negate it, as in 不爱.
- `Yes/no questions with 吗`: Add 吗 at the end of a statement to turn it into a yes/no question.
- `Plural pronouns with 们`: The lesson introduces plural pronouns by adding 们 to pronoun bases.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `你好`: Practice third-tone sandhi: 你 is pronounced more like a rising tone before 好.
- `谢谢`: Keep the x sound light and fronted; the second syllable is shorter and lighter.
- `再见`: Keep both syllables clipped and clear; avoid turning 再见 into a Russian-style diphthong.
- `吗`: Treat 吗 as an unstressed question particle at the end of the sentence.
- `你呢`: Keep 呢 very light; the weight stays on 你 and the whole phrase should sound short and conversational.

## Drill Preview

- `repeat`: Repeat the greeting exchange: 你好. 你好吗? 我很好.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I love you.
- `substitution`: Swap pronouns to say: You love me.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: They do not love us.
- `roleplay`: Roleplay a polite closing: thank the listener and say goodbye.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 你好吗？ mean?
- `recall`: Which particle turns a statement into a yes/no question in this lesson?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: And you?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: Thank you all.
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: He loves you.

## Teaching Notes

- Open with the etiquette loop first: 你好 -> 你好吗？ -> 我很好 -> 谢谢 -> 再见.
- Keep the first grammar frame extremely narrow: pronouns + 爱 + object.
- Use lesson 01 to automate word order and particles before adding more verbs.
- Do not overload Hanzi writing yet; focus on recognition, pronunciation, and oral pattern control.

# Lesson 02: Question Words, Identity, and Place


## Vocabulary Preview

- `什么` `shenme` -> what; which
- `谁` `shei` -> who
- `哪儿` `nar` -> where; where to
- `在` `zai` -> to be located in; at
- `是` `shi` -> to be; to be identified as
- `叫` `jiao` -> to be called; to be named
- `名字` `mingzi` -> name
- `住` `zhu` -> to live; reside

## Grammar Preview

- `Question words stay in slot`: Question words such as 什么, 谁, and 哪儿 stay in the same place as the information they replace.
- `Copula 是 for identity`: Use 是 to identify who someone is, what nationality they are, or what profession they have.
- `Name pattern with 叫`: Use 叫 to ask and answer names: 你叫什么名字? / 我叫....
- `Residence with 住在`: Use 住 plus 在 plus a place to say where someone lives.
- `Nationality pattern with 哪国人`: Ask nationality with 你是哪国人 and answer with a country plus 人.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `什么`: Keep the second syllable in 什么 light and unstressed; the question word should not sound like two equal Russian stresses.
- `谁 and 是`: Separate 谁 and 是 clearly; Russian ears often blur these into one fuzzy sh-sound cluster.
- `哪儿 / 在哪儿`: Keep 哪儿 short and conversational inside 在哪儿; do not over-stress the final r-colouring.
- `老师`: Practice the tone contour lao3 shi1 as a stable address form after a surname.
- `工作`: Keep 工作 clipped and rhythmic; avoid flattening the first syllable into Russian-style stress timing.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: What is your name?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I live in Moscow.
- `substitution`: Swap the country and ask: Are you Russian?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: What work do you do?
- `roleplay`: Roleplay a short self-introduction: name, city, nationality, and profession.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 你住在哪儿？ mean?
- `recall`: Which word in lesson 02 means 'to be called; to be named'?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: What country are you from?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: I am a singer.
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: Thank you, teacher.

## Teaching Notes

- Open lesson 02 by reviewing only one grammar expansion at a time: question words, then name, then residence, then nationality, then work.
- Keep student output in very short clauses before combining them into mini self-introductions.
- Use profession words only if they matter to the learner; the lesson itself models this selective vocabulary strategy.
- When drilling nationality and residence, keep the frames separate: 我住在... versus 我是...人.
- Use 老师 as an address form after a surname only after learners can already control the tones.

# Lesson 03: Core Verbs, Wants, and Food/Drink Choices


## Vocabulary Preview

- `问` `wen` -> to ask
- `看` `kan` -> to look at; to watch
- `听` `ting` -> to listen
- `吃` `chi` -> to eat
- `喝` `he` -> to drink
- `想` `xiang` -> to want; to think; to miss
- `什么` `shenme` -> what
- `请` `qing` -> please

## Grammar Preview

- `New verbs keep the same pronoun frame`: Lesson 03 shows that once the pronoun order is stable, new verbs such as 问, 看, and 听 drop into the same subject-verb-object pattern.
- `Content question versus 吗 question`: Use question words like 谁 or 什么 when asking for missing information, and use 吗 only for yes-no questions.
- `想 + verb + object`: To say what someone wants right now, place 想 before the action: 想喝, 想吃, or 想喝水.
- `有 / 没有 for possession`: Use 有 to say that someone has a family member and 没有 to say that they do not.
- `Family possession can omit 的`: With close family members, 的 is often optional, so the lesson allows compact forms like 我妈妈.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `问 / 听`: Keep the final nasal clear: 问 has a short -n ending, while 听 carries the longer -ng ending.
- `水`: Keep 水 as one compact syllable ending in -ui; do not split it into two Russian-style beats.
- `有 / 没有`: The lesson highlights tone behavior around 有 and 没有, so practice them as a fixed pair rather than isolated syllables.
- `和 versus 喝`: 和 'and' and 喝 'drink' sound close to beginners, so keep them distinct by tone and sentence role.
- `一 and 十`: The ending number block trains clean tone contrast between 一 and 十, which beginners often flatten together.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: What do you want to drink?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I do not drink tea. I drink coffee.
- `roleplay`: Introduce your family in three short clauses using 有 or 没有.
- `translation`: Ask in Chinese: Where does your family live?
- `repeat`: Count from one to ten aloud with the teacher.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 你看谁？ mean?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: I want to drink water.
- `recall`: Which pair expresses have / do not have in lesson 03?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: My father is not a doctor.
- `speaking`: Count from one to ten in Chinese.

## Teaching Notes

- Start lesson 03 by reusing lesson 01-02 pronouns before introducing any new verb; the point is that the frame stays stable while the verb changes.
- Keep 想 drills practical: ask only about tea, coffee, water, or food that the learner can answer immediately.
- Use family nouns selectively. The lesson itself warns that the extended kinship tree becomes too complex too quickly.
- When the learner confuses 和 and 喝, switch from meaning to tone contrast, then put both back into full sentences.
- Close the session by counting aloud with body rhythm or finger taps so the number block becomes automatic instead of purely visual.

# Lesson 04: Numbers, Weekdays, Months, and Birthdays


## Vocabulary Preview

- `二十` `er shi` -> twenty
- `三十` `san shi` -> thirty
- `四十` `si shi` -> forty
- `五十` `wu shi` -> fifty
- `六十` `liu shi` -> sixty
- `七十` `qi shi` -> seventy
- `八十` `ba shi` -> eighty
- `九十` `jiu shi` -> ninety

## Grammar Preview

- `Tens are built with numeral plus 十`: Lesson 04 extends counting by stacking a numeral with 十, then adding another numeral if needed.
- `Months are number plus 月`: Chinese month names are transparent: the numeral comes first, then 月.
- `Weekdays use 星期 plus a number`: Most weekdays are built from 星期 and a numeral, while Sunday uses 天 instead of a numeral.
- `Dates use month plus 号`: When you say a date, put the month first and the day number before 号.
- `Years are read digit by digit plus 年`: Instead of saying thousands and hundreds as in Russian, the lesson reads each year digit in sequence and adds 年.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `号 versus 好`: The lesson explicitly contrasts 号 for dates with 好 for good, so the learner should practice them as a tone pair, not as one memorized spelling.
- `月`: Keep 月 compact with the fronted yue vowel; do not flatten it into a Russian-style ue sequence.
- `星期一 / 星期天`: Weekday phrases should stay rhythmic as one unit; do not over-stress the middle syllables.
- `点`: Practice 点 as the clock-time marker inside a full phrase such as 十点 or 八点吃饭.
- `什么时候`: Keep 什么时候 fluent as a single question chunk; beginners often break it into too many stressed pieces.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: What month and what date is it?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: When is your birthday?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I drink coffee at ten o'clock.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: April fourth.
- `repeat`: Count by tens from twenty to one hundred.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 星期一 mean?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: See you tomorrow.
- `recall`: Which word in lesson 04 means zero?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: My older sister's birthday is September fourth.
- `speaking`: Say the time phrase: I eat at eight o'clock.

## Teaching Notes

- Keep lesson 04 practical by tying every number to a date, month, weekday, year, or clock time instead of chanting numbers in isolation for too long.
- Use the birthday block as the main speaking loop: ask month, day, and year only after the learner can already ask when the birthday is.
- Whenever the learner forgets word order, return to the simple template: subject, then time, then action.
- Contrast 号 and 好 out loud early; the lesson itself highlights this because beginners confuse them immediately.
- End the session with a real scheduling exchange such as 明天见 or 星期一见 so the calendar vocabulary becomes social, not just mechanical.

# Lesson 05: Larger Numbers, Direction, and Going Somewhere


## Vocabulary Preview

- `非常好` `feichang hao` -> excellent; extremely good
- `百` `bai` -> hundred
- `零` `ling` -> zero
- `千` `qian` -> thousand
- `钱` `qian` -> money
- `为什么` `weishenme` -> why
- `在` `zai` -> in; at
- `去` `qu` -> to go

## Grammar Preview

- `Hundreds and thousands use 百 and 千`: Lesson 05 extends counting by stacking numeral units such as 百 and 千 after the leading number.
- `零 fills missing place values`: When a complex number skips a place value, 零 keeps the structure clear, as in one hundred one.
- `Directional verbs 去 and 来`: Use 去 for movement away from the speaker and 来 for movement toward the speaker.
- `Completed action with 了`: Add 了 when the lesson wants to show that a motion event has already happened.
- `Subject, time, action, destination`: The lesson keeps returning to the same order: subject first, then time, then the action, then where the movement goes.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `千 vs 钱`: The lesson pairs 千 and 钱 to show that the syllable stays similar while the tone changes the meaning completely.
- `喜欢`: Keep the second syllable of 喜欢 lighter and less stressed; Russian speakers often over-hit the end of the word.
- `会说`: Practice 会说 as one smooth chunk because the lesson uses it as the core language-ability frame.
- `城市`: Keep 城市 rhythmic as a two-syllable noun rather than giving both syllables equal Russian-style weight.
- `回来 / 回家`: Distinguish 回来 from 回家 by keeping the second syllable clear; one is back here, the other is back home.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I want to go to China.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I like Moscow.
- `translation`: Ask in Chinese: What is this?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I understand Chinese, but I cannot speak it.
- `repeat`: Repeat the number pattern: one hundred one; one hundred ten; one thousand.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 喜欢 mean?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: Which country have you been to?
- `recall`: Which word in lesson 05 means thousand?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: What language can you speak?
- `speaking`: Say in Chinese: I go home at eight o'clock.

## Teaching Notes

- Do not let lesson 05 become only a number chant. Tie the hundred and thousand patterns immediately to phone numbers, dates, or place counts.
- The movement block is the real hinge of the lesson: contrast 去, 来, 回, 回来, and 回家 until the learner can feel the direction without translation.
- Use 喜欢 plus cities or countries for natural conversational output instead of abstract geography quizzes.
- When the learner reaches language names, switch from bare 说 to 会说 so ability and action stay distinct.
- End on one real personal sentence with time and motion, such as 我八点回家 or 我明天去中国, to reinforce the subject-time-action pattern.

# Lesson 06: Time of Day, Knowing, and Speaking Languages


## Vocabulary Preview

- `当然` `dangran` -> of course
- `早` `zao` -> early
- `晚` `wan` -> late
- `早上` `zaoshang` -> morning
- `晚上` `wanshang` -> evening
- `知道` `zhidao` -> to know
- `不知道` `bu zhidao` -> not know; I do not know
- `你知道吗？` `ni zhidao ma?` -> Do you know?

## Grammar Preview

- `Time roots become parts of day with 上`: Lesson 06 builds morning and evening by taking simple time roots and combining them into 早上 and 晚上.
- `知道 and 吗 build a practical check-in question`: The class turns 知道 into a dialogue tool by asking 你知道吗 and answering with 知道 or 不知道.
- `会说 plus a language`: To express language ability, lesson 06 keeps using 会 before 说 and then adds the language name.
- `明白了 marks a completed change of understanding`: When the learner has just understood something, 了 marks that change of state, as in 我明白了.
- `不 versus 没 for present and past negation`: The lesson explicitly contrasts 不 for current or habitual negation and 没 for past actions that did not happen.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `当然`: Keep 当然 as a smooth two-syllable chunk; Russian speakers often over-separate the second syllable.
- `早上 / 晚上`: Practice 早上 and 晚上 as time-of-day bundles rather than stressing each character like a list.
- `明白了`: Do not flatten the final particle in 明白了; the sentence should still sound like a completed realization rather than a plain dictionary form.
- `对不起`: Treat 对不起 as one apology formula. Beginners often pause after 对, which makes it sound fragmented.
- `不客气`: Keep 不客气 light and brisk as a response formula, not as three isolated dictionary words.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: Do you know?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I cannot speak Chinese; I study Chinese.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: What did you do yesterday evening?
- `repeat`: Repeat the greeting pair: good morning / good evening.
- `roleplay`: Roleplay the food exchange: ask whether someone likes Chinese cuisine and whether they can cook it.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 当然 mean?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: I do not know.
- `recall`: Which negative particle does lesson 06 use for past actions that did not happen?
- `speaking`: Say the cultural check-in phrase: Have you eaten?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: Sorry. It is okay. You are welcome.

## Teaching Notes

- Keep lesson 06 practical by returning to already-known verbs and only then layering in new time words and social formulas.
- Use the contrast between 不 and 没 as the main grammar loop; it is the most useful new distinction in this lesson.
- Do not teach 吃饭, 喝茶, 睡觉, and 做饭 as isolated verbs. Keep them as whole action chunks.
- The native-speaker drill is best used after the learner can already answer three safe questions: 你知道吗, 你今天晚上做什么, and 你喜欢吃中国菜吗.
- End the session with apology and thanks responses so the learner leaves with polite survival language, not only classroom grammar.

# Lesson 07: How, Can, and Time-Sensitive Questions


## Vocabulary Preview

- `怎么` `zenme` -> how
- `不怎么样` `bu zenmeyang` -> not so well; so-so
- `还` `hai` -> still; rather
- `可以` `keyi` -> can; may; okay
- `还可以` `hai keyi` -> not bad; okay
- `来` `lai` -> to come
- `看` `kan` -> to look; to watch; to read
- `了` `le` -> completed-result particle

## Grammar Preview

- `怎么 asks how`: Lesson 07 adds 怎么 so the learner can ask how someone is or how something went instead of repeating only what and who questions.
- `可以 marks possibility or permission`: The class contrasts 可以 with 会: 可以 means can or may in the sense of possibility or permission, not learned ability.
- `Context often carries time in Chinese`: If the sentence already names yesterday, today, tomorrow, or now, the teacher treats the time reference as sufficiently established without extra tense marking.
- `Verb matrix: plain, negated, completed, experiential, and future-intention forms`: The core board work is a verb matrix around 看: plain 我看, negated 我不看, question 我看吗, completed 我看了, experiential 我看过, past negation 我没看, never-experienced 我没看过, and future intention 我要看. The lesson also says future can often be carried by context such as tomorrow.
- `了 versus 过`: 了 marks a completed result or changed state, while 过 marks the fact of having had an experience before.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `怎么 / 怎么样`: Keep 怎么 and 怎么样 flowing as compact question chunks; beginners often over-separate them syllable by syllable.
- `可以`: Do not confuse 可以 with 会. In this lesson 可以 should sound like a permission-or-possibility chunk, not like the ability verb from earlier lessons.
- `看了 / 看过`: Practice 看了 and 看过 back to back so the learner hears the different aspect meaning while keeping the verb stem stable.
- `吃了吗`: Treat 吃了吗 as a natural greeting chunk instead of stressing every syllable equally.
- `还是`: Keep 还是 light and connective inside a choice question rather than turning it into a heavily stressed standalone word.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: How are you now?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: Can you come today?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I have never seen it.
- `repeat`: Repeat the greeting pair: Have you eaten? I have eaten.
- `roleplay`: Roleplay the choice question: ask whether someone prefers eating at home or in a restaurant.

## Test Preview

- `recognition`: What does 还可以 mean in this lesson?
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: Have you been to China?
- `recall`: Which particle in lesson 07 marks bare experience rather than a completed result?
- `speaking`: Say the sentence: I went to visit friends yesterday.
- `translation`: Translate into Chinese: I do not like drinking tea.

## Teaching Notes

- Keep lesson 07 anchored in the Petrov-inspired verb matrix. The main value is not individual words alone, but the contrast between plain, completed, experiential, future/intention, and negated forms.
- Use 看 as the main demo verb first, then swap in other known verbs like 去, 吃, and 喝 only after the pattern feels stable.
- When learners over-focus on tense, return them to the lesson's central idea: Chinese often lets the time phrase and context carry the time reference.
- Teach 吃了吗 and 还可以 as social chunks, not as purely analytical grammar examples. They make the learner sound natural immediately.
- Save the movie-country drill for the end of the session; it is a good way to recycle 还是, 看电影, and country adjectives after the heavier grammar block.

# Lesson 08: Meeting People, Age, and Quantity Questions


## Vocabulary Preview

- `高兴` `gaoxing` -> glad; happy
- `知道` `zhidao` -> to know, in the sense of having information
- `认识` `renshi` -> to know someone; to meet; to become acquainted
- `高兴认识你` `gaoxing renshi ni` -> glad to meet you
- `岁` `sui` -> years old
- `多 / 少` `duo / shao` -> many / few; much / little
- `多少` `duoshao` -> how much; how many
- `大 / 小` `da / xiao` -> big / small

## Grammar Preview

- `认识 versus 知道`: Lesson 08 distinguishes knowing information from knowing a person. 知道 is informational knowledge, while 认识 is acquaintance or getting acquainted.
- `Age uses 岁, not 年`: To say how old a person is, Chinese uses 岁 directly. The teacher explicitly contrasts this with 年 as a year number.
- `Quantity question with 多少`: The class builds 'how much / how many' by merging the words for much and little into 多少.
- `Age question with 多大`: For a general age question, lesson 08 uses 多大, literally 'how big'.
- `Result complements with 到`: Adding 到 turns basic action verbs into result forms such as seeing, hearing, or finding successfully.

## Pronunciation Preview

- `高兴认识你`: Treat 高兴认识你 as a smooth social chunk. If you pause after each syllable, it stops sounding like a greeting.
- `多少 / 多大`: Keep 多少 and 多大 distinct: both start with 多, but the second syllable changes the meaning completely.
- `告诉`: Do not clip 告诉 into separate dictionary syllables. It should feel like one quick command chunk in 请告诉我.
- `睡觉 / 起床`: Practice 睡觉 and 起床 as daily-routine units; they sound more natural as ready-made actions than as decomposed characters.
- `的时候`: 的时候 should sound like a clause linker, not like a stop-start string of isolated syllables.

## Drill Preview

- `translation`: Say in Chinese: I am glad to meet you.
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: How old are you?
- `translation`: Say in Chinese: Please tell me something.

- Keep why-because, favorite color, and music preference short. They are there to keep a conversation alive, not to turn into abstract explanation blocks.
- The closing reflection matters. It explains that the real gain is not perfect recall but the feeling that the language has stopped being alien.
- Do not overclean the final chorus. 谢谢老师 is simple, repetitive, and emotionally appropriate exactly as it appears.
