# Lesson 04: Numbers, Weekdays, Months, and Birthdays

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Tens are built with numeral plus 十, Months are number plus 月, and Weekdays use 星期 plus a number and usable language includes twenty, thirty, forty, and fifty.

## Conspect Notes

- Keep lesson 04 practical by tying every number to a date, month, weekday, year, or clock time instead of chanting numbers in isolation for too long.
- Use the birthday block as the main speaking loop: ask month, day, and year only after the learner can already ask when the birthday is.
- Whenever the learner forgets word order, return to the simple template: subject, then time, then action.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Tens are built with numeral plus 十, Months are number plus 月, and Weekdays use 星期 plus a number and turn 二十 (twenty), 三十 (thirty), and 四十 (forty) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Petrov Stage

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 你的生日什么时候？ — ni de shengri shenme shihou? — When is your birthday?
- 你朋友什么时候过生日？ — ni pengyou shenme shihou guo shengri? — When does your friend celebrate their birthday?
- 她什么时候过生日？ — ta shenme shihou guo shengri? — When is her birthday?
- 什么时候过生日？ — shenme shihou guo shengri? — When is the birthday / when do they celebrate the birthday?
- 你朋友什么时候喝咖啡？ — ni pengyou shenme shihou he kafei? — What time does your friend drink coffee?

## Conversational Mission

Say a date, weekday, and birthday-style calendar line without reading.

## Correction Priorities

- 号 versus 好: The lesson explicitly contrasts 号 for dates with 好 for good, so the learner should practice them as a tone pair, not as one memorized spelling.
- 月: Keep 月 compact with the fronted yue vowel; do not flatten it into a Russian-style ue sequence.
- Keep lesson 04 practical by tying every number to a date, month, weekday, year, or clock time instead of chanting numbers in isolation for too long.
- Use the birthday block as the main speaking loop: ask month, day, and year only after the learner can already ask when the birthday is.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
