# Lesson 09: Greetings, Demonstratives, Classifiers, and Comparison

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Demonstratives and numbers need a classifier, 个 is the default classifier, but some nouns use more specific ones, and Comparison with 比 and usable language includes good morning, good evening, hello, and long time no see.

## Conspect Notes

- Do not rush past the opening etiquette review. 好久不见 gives the learner a more natural social entry point than repeating only 你好.
- Teach classifiers as a structural habit, not a list to memorize in isolation: 个 first, then 杯 and 口 as memorable exceptions.
- Use 比, 太, and 最 together so the learner feels one comparison system rather than three disconnected words.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Demonstratives and numbers need a classifier, 个 is the default classifier, but some nouns use more specific ones, and Comparison with 比 and turn 早上好 (good morning), 晚上好 (good evening), and 你好 (hello) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Petrov Stage

Stabilize demonstratives, classifiers, and comparison inside everyday talk.

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 你家有几口人？ — ni jia you ji kou ren? — How many people are there in your family?
- 多少？ — duoshao? — How much?
- 你喜欢什么颜色？ — ni xihuan shenme yanse? — What color do you like?

- 你喜欢什么颜色的衣服？ — ni xihuan shenme yanse de yifu? — What color clothes do you like?

## Correction Priorities

- 好久不见: Keep 好久不见 as one social chunk. The lesson explicitly contrasts 久 'long time' with 九 'nine', so tone clarity matters.
- 个, 杯, and 干杯: Do not blur 个 and 杯 into the same vowel sound. 干杯 should sound like a toast chunk, not two dictionary syllables.
- Do not rush past the opening etiquette review. 好久不见 gives the learner a more natural social entry point than repeating only 你好.
- Teach classifiers as a structural habit, not a list to memorize in isolation: 个 first, then 杯 and 口 as memorable exceptions.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
