# Lesson 03: Core Verbs, Wants, and Food/Drink Choices

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on New verbs keep the same pronoun frame, Content question versus 吗 question, and 想 + verb + object and usable language includes to ask, to look at; to watch, to listen, and to eat.

## Conspect Notes

- Start lesson 03 by reusing lesson 01-02 pronouns before introducing any new verb; the point is that the frame stays stable while the verb changes.
- Keep 想 drills practical: ask only about tea, coffee, water, or food that the learner can answer immediately.
- Use family nouns selectively. The lesson itself warns that the extended kinship tree becomes too complex too quickly.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate New verbs keep the same pronoun frame, Content question versus 吗 question, and 想 + verb + object and turn 问 (to ask), 看 (to look at; to watch), and 听 (to listen) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Petrov Stage

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 你问我？ — ni wen wo? — Are you asking me?
- 你看谁？ — ni kan shei? — Who are you looking at?
- 你看吗？ — ni kan ma? — Are you watching / looking?
- 你听我吗？ — ni ting wo ma? — Are you listening to me?
- 你不听我吗？ — ni bu ting wo ma? — Are you not listening to me?

## Petrov Lesson Flow

- Fast return: recover Question words stay in slot and Copula 是 for identity before adding anything new.
- Narrow introduction: open only New verbs keep the same pronoun frame, Content question versus 吗 question, and 想 + verb + object and keep the rest stable.
- Teacher ladder: move through 你问我？ — ni wen wo? — Are you asking me?, 你看谁？ — ni kan shei? — Who are you looking at?, and 你看吗？ — ni kan ma? — Are you watching / looking? one line at a time.
- Pattern rotation: run Say in Chinese: What do you want to drink?
- Personal answer loop: Ask what someone wants to eat or drink and answer with one clear preference.
- Acceleration: Say in Chinese: I do not drink tea. I drink coffee.

## Conversational Mission

Ask what someone wants to eat or drink and answer with one clear preference.

## Correction Priorities

- 问 / 听: Keep the final nasal clear: 问 has a short -n ending, while 听 carries the longer -ng ending.
- 水: Keep 水 as one compact syllable ending in -ui; do not split it into two Russian-style beats.
- Start lesson 03 by reusing lesson 01-02 pronouns before introducing any new verb; the point is that the frame stays stable while the verb changes.
- Keep 想 drills practical: ask only about tea, coffee, water, or food that the learner can answer immediately.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
