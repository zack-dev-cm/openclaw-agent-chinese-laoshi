# Lesson 15: Seasons, Etiquette, Formal Address, and Family

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on Seasons are built with 天, Etiquette phrases are the true language core, and Polite 您 replaces informal 你 in formal address and usable language includes spring, summer, autumn, and winter.

## Conspect Notes

- Do not sell lesson 15 as one narrow topic. It is a consolidation lesson that intentionally braids seasons, etiquette, guest interaction, frequency adverbs, and family language together.
- Keep the etiquette block near-automatic. If the learner still has to think hard about hello, sorry, thank you, and welcome, the later conversation work will stay brittle.
- Use the guest dialogue as confidence training, not as a museum piece. The point is that the students really ask and answer simple but real questions.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate Seasons are built with 天, Etiquette phrases are the true language core, and Polite 您 replaces informal 你 in formal address and turn 春天 (spring), 夏天 (summer), and 秋天 (autumn) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Correction Priorities

- 春天 / 秋天 / 冬天 / 夏天: Drill the four seasons as a compact set so the repeated 天 ending stays stable while the first syllable changes.
- 对不起 / 不客气: These etiquette phrases should come out as full chunks, not word-by-word constructions.
- Do not sell lesson 15 as one narrow topic. It is a consolidation lesson that intentionally braids seasons, etiquette, guest interaction, frequency adverbs, and family language together.
- Keep the etiquette block near-automatic. If the learner still has to think hard about hello, sorry, thank you, and welcome, the later conversation work will stay brittle.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
