# Lesson 01: Pronouns, Greetings, and Basic Negation

## Lesson Summary

In this lesson, the lesson centers on SVO word order, No case endings on pronouns, and Negation with 不 and usable language includes i; me, you, he; she, and we; us.

## Conspect Notes

- Open with the etiquette loop first: 你好 -> 你好吗？ -> 我很好 -> 谢谢 -> 再见.
- Keep the first grammar frame extremely narrow: pronouns + 爱 + object.
- Use lesson 01 to automate word order and particles before adding more verbs.

## Teacher Goal

Use the lesson to automate SVO word order, No case endings on pronouns, and Negation with 不 and turn 我 (i; me), 你 (you), and 他 / 她 (he; she) into live spoken material.

## Student Outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should complete the lesson mission without reading and reuse at least one current pattern in a new personal line.

## Petrov Stage

Build the first stable pronoun matrix and automate yes/no etiquette lines.

## Petrov Continuity

- Start with the smallest stable matrix: pronouns, greeting etiquette, 吗 questions, and negation.
- Do not broaden topics yet. The goal is to make the very first sentence frame feel non-alien.

## Teacher Question Ladder

- 你好吗？ — ni hao ma? — How are you?
- 你呢？ — ni ne? — And you?
- 你爱我吗？ — ni ai wo ma? — Do you love me?
- 爱不爱？ — ai bu ai? — love or not? / do ... love?
- 你们好 / 你好吗？ / 我很好 / 谢谢 / 你呢？ — ni men hao / ni hao ma? / wo hen hao / xiexie / ni ne? — Hello, everyone. How are you? I am fine. Thank you. And you?

## Petrov Lesson Flow

- Fast return: recover the previous lesson core before adding anything new.
- Narrow introduction: open only SVO word order, No case endings on pronouns, and Negation with 不 and keep the rest stable.
- Teacher ladder: move through 你好吗？ — ni hao ma? — How are you?, 你呢？ — ni ne? — And you?, and 你爱我吗？ — ni ai wo ma? — Do you love me? one line at a time.
- Pattern rotation: run Repeat the greeting exchange: 你好. 你好吗? 我很好.
- Personal answer loop: Greet someone, introduce yourself, and give one short positive or negative statement.
- Acceleration: Say in Chinese: I love you.

## Conversational Mission

Greet someone, introduce yourself, and give one short positive or negative statement.

## Correction Priorities

- 你好: Practice third-tone sandhi: 你 is pronounced more like a rising tone before 好.
- 谢谢: Keep the x sound light and fronted; the second syllable is shorter and lighter.
- Open with the etiquette loop first: 你好 -> 你好吗？ -> 我很好 -> 谢谢 -> 再见.
- Keep the first grammar frame extremely narrow: pronouns + 爱 + object.
- If the learner breaks the frame, shrink back to one stable sentence and rebuild one variable at a time.
